Sentiometer Mac Visualizer
==========================

What's in this folder
---------------------
  Sentiometer Visualizer.command   ← double-click this to launch
  sentiometer_viz.py               ← the visualizer code (single file)
  sentiometer.yaml                 ← device settings (don't edit)
  READ ME FIRST.txt                ← you are here


How to launch (first time)
--------------------------
1. Plug the Sentiometer into a USB port. macOS will pop up a dialog:
       "Do you want to connect STMicroelectronics BLACKPILL_F401CC CDC
        in FS Mode to this Mac?"
   Click Allow.

2. RIGHT-click "Sentiometer Visualizer.command" and choose Open.
       (DON'T just double-click the first time — macOS Gatekeeper
        will block apps downloaded from the internet on a plain
        double-click. Right-click → Open works around that, and you
        only need to do it once.)

3. A confirmation dialog appears: "macOS cannot verify the developer
   of …". Click Open.

4. A Terminal window opens and runs the launcher. The first run will:
     • Install a tool called `uv` (~10 MB, from astral.sh)
     • Download matplotlib + numpy + pyserial into an isolated env
     • Open the connect dialog
   Total time: about 30–60 seconds, depending on your connection.

5. The connect dialog shows your serial ports. The Sentiometer is
   marked with a star (★) and is auto-selected. Click Connect.

6. After ~3 seconds (the device firmware needs a moment to start),
   the live visualization window opens.


How to launch (every other time)
--------------------------------
Just double-click "Sentiometer Visualizer.command". It should launch
in 1–2 seconds — `uv` caches everything from the first run.


Using the visualizer
--------------------
Left side  — six stacked plots:
  Mean        Average of all 5 photodiodes (PD1–PD5).
              Two overlays: 10 s SMA in red, 30 s SMA in blue.
  PD1–PD5     The raw photodiode channels.
  Each shows the most recent ~5 seconds, autoscaled.

Right side — analysis panel. The button at the bottom cycles through
three views:
  1. Band power (EEG)   Delta / Theta / Alpha / Beta / Gamma bars.
  2. Power spectrum     Full PSD line, 0–250 Hz.
  3. Band power (All)   20 ten-Hz-wide bars covering 0–200 Hz.

Window text box — type a number (1–60) and press Enter to change how
many seconds of data feed the analysis panel. Default is 10 s.

To stop: close either window.


Troubleshooting
---------------
"No USB serial devices found"
    The Mac doesn't see the device. Unplug, wait 2 s, plug back in.
    Make sure you clicked Allow on the macOS connect prompt.

"No valid samples received within 60 seconds"
    The device opened but didn't start streaming. Unplug the USB cable,
    wait 5 s, plug it back in, and click Connect again.

"command not found: uv" (after first run)
    Close the Terminal window and re-launch. `uv` was just installed
    and the new PATH only takes effect in a fresh shell.

Visualizer feels slow / drops samples
    Other USB-heavy apps (CoolTerm, Arduino IDE, etc.) can compete for
    the port. Close them and re-launch.


Where the libraries actually live
---------------------------------
`uv` keeps everything in ~/.local/share/uv (the install) and a tiny
cache. To completely uninstall: drop this folder, then run in Terminal:
    rm -rf ~/.local/share/uv ~/.cache/uv
That's it — nothing else changes on your system.
